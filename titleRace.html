Title Race



The race for the title has heated up this season with Manchester City defending the title from the european champions Liverpool FC. With City having stregthening with the signings of Rodri and Cancelo whilst Liverpool being inactive in the transfer market, it seems the league has been wrapped up by the defeding champions.


Before the transfer market opened it was mind boggling to predict the 3rd and 4th places finishes. With Tottenham and Arsenal able to secure lucrative deals, it is easy to conclude that both Manchester United and Chelsea will be missing out on the Chapions League qualification spots.


The Champions of Europe having taken all 24 points from 8 games, and Man City only managing to squeeze out 16 points from the same time frame. We can expect the the league to pan out more more realistcly around Christmas time.


The Deciding Factor



In my opinion the title race for the Premier League comes down to games between Man City and Liverpool. This is because the were a mere 1 point gap between the winners and the runners up last season.


Both Anfield (Liverpool's stadium) and Etihad (Man City's stadium) are both known to be the fotress of both teams, and it will be fascinating to see who comes out on top on the respective games. Anfield is set to host Manchester City on the 10th November 2019 and the reverse fixture is scheduled to play on the 4th April 2020.
